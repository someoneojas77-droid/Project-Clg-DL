# Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

## What is UPI Fraud?
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of cybercrime where 
attackers trick victims into authorising fraudulent transactions 
through deception, fake links, or social engineering.

## How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)
1. The attacker contacts the victim via phone, WhatsApp, or SMS 
   pretending to be a bank representative, seller, or government official.
2. They claim the victim has won a prize, needs to receive a refund, 
   or must verify their account urgently.
3. The attacker sends a fake UPI payment REQUEST (not a send link) — 
   the victim mistakes it for receiving money and enters their UPI PIN.
4. The moment the victim enters their PIN, money is DEDUCTED from 
   their account — not deposited.
5. The attacker disappears and blocks the victim immediately.

## Who is Usually Targeted?
- First-time UPI users and senior citizens unfamiliar with digital 
  payments
- College students buying or selling items on OLX, Facebook 
  Marketplace, or Instagram
- People expecting refunds or cashbacks from online shopping

## Real Scenario
In 2023, a college student in Pune listed his old laptop for sale on 
OLX. A buyer contacted him and said he would send ₹15,000 via UPI. 
He sent a "collect request" of ₹15,000 instead. The student, 
thinking he was receiving money, entered his UPI PIN — and lost 
₹15,000 instantly. The buyer then blocked him on all platforms.

## Consequences
- Immediate financial loss with very low chance of recovery
- Psychological stress and loss of trust in digital payments
- Legal complications if the fraud involves large amounts

Source: Based on reported cases from cybercrime.gov.in and 
news reports from The Hindu and Times of India (2023)
